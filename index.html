What is the Death Spiral? Peloton's platform carries large fixed costs (subscription content, instructor salaries, technology infrastructure, S&M, G&A, R&D) that do not scale down when subscribers leave. As paid connected fitness subscribers decline from their peak, those fixed costs must be absorbed by a smaller subscriber base, raising the effective cost-per-subscriber. Higher unit costs compress margins, potentially forcing price increases or content cuts — which drive further churn. This is a demand-driven death spiral.

Baseline (FY2025 10-K, June 30, 2025): 2.80M ending paid connected fitness subscribers  ·  ~$1,673.7M annual subscription revenue  ·  1.6% avg monthly churn  ·  ~$1,699.7M estimated annual fixed platform costs (Sub CoR $516.6M + S&M $421.6M + G&A $527.3M + R&D $234.2M)  10-K FY2025

🌀 Death Spiral Stage Indicator
1
Demand Shock — Subscriber base begins declining from 2.80M FY2025 baseline. Fixed platform costs ($1,699.7M) remain largely unchanged.
2
Cost Dilution — Fixed costs spread over fewer subscribers, pushing cost-per-sub above the $44/mo price point. Contribution margin turns negative.
3
Margin Compression — Subscription gross margin deteriorates. Management faces pressure to raise prices or cut content quality to reduce the $516.6M subscription cost base.
4
Churn Acceleration — Price hikes or content cuts (instructor layoffs, fewer live classes) trigger additional cancellations, compounding the subscriber decline beyond the 1.6% monthly churn rate.
5
Structural Crisis — Platform becomes economically unviable at current cost structure. Covenant risk emerges: the $1.2B trailing subscription revenue covenant on the $990M Term Loan is threatened.

✅ Manageable — Monitoring Required

At current scenario parameters, the platform is under pressure but not yet in a structural death spiral. The 1.6% monthly churn rate and $1,699.7M fixed cost base require close monitoring. Peloton's $990M Term Loan includes a $1.2B trailing subscription revenue covenant — current trajectory puts this at risk if subscriber decline accelerates.
